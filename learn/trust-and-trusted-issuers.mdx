---
title: "Trust and trusted issuers"
description: "Learn why verifiers need to know which issuers to believe."
---

A verifier is the system checking a credential. An issuer is the organization that created it. A verifier should not accept every signed credential.

A fake company can sign a fake student ID, just like anyone can print a badge at home. The signature proves who made the credential, but the verifier still needs to decide whether that issuer is allowed.

Think of a bouncer at a club. The bouncer does not accept every card with a photo. The bouncer has a list of ID issuers that count, such as a driver's license agency or passport office.

In Didit ID, that list is the trust registry. A trusted issuer is one issuer on that list. If the issuer is not trusted, the credential can be rejected even if the digital seal is valid.

## Trust registry

A trust registry is the verifier's list of issuers it accepts. It answers the question, "Who do we believe for this kind of proof?"

For example, an airport gate may trust the airline for boarding pass credentials. It should not trust a random website that claims to issue boarding passes.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose the trust context">
    Pick the trust framework, which is the rulebook or ecosystem for the kind of credential you want to verify.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add trusted issuers">
    Add each issuer you accept. This is like adding approved ID agencies to the bouncer's list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify presentations">
    When a wallet presents a credential, Didit ID checks the digital seal and whether the credential is still active. It also checks whether the issuer is trusted.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  A credential can have a valid digital seal and still fail because the issuer is not in your trust registry.
</Note>

## Trust frameworks

A trust framework is a named rulebook for a credential ecosystem. It helps you group trust decisions under a shared policy.

Electronic Identification, Authentication and Trust Services (eIDAS) is a European Union trust framework for electronic identity and trust services.

Mobile driving license (mDL) is a trust framework family for digital driver's licenses based on mobile license standards.

<Tip>
  Behind the scenes, Didit ID uses a few agreed-upon technical formats — **you don't need to know their names to use it.** They are just industry standards that let different wallets and apps work together: the credential format is SD-JWT VC, issuing uses OpenID4VCI, and presenting uses OpenID4VP. When you're curious what each one means, the [Concepts](/concepts/sd-jwt-vc) pages explain them.
</Tip>
